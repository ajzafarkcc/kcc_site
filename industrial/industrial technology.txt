<img class="alignright size-full wp-image-35882" src="https://www.kellogg.edu/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/RMTC-Industrial-Tech-lab-11-25-19.jpg" alt="A KCC Industrial Electricity/Electronics student works with electrical equipment at the RMTC." width="430" height="287">The Industrial Technology program at Kellogg Community College fosters the ingenuity of students who like taking things apart. This program hinges on the complex problem solving involved in the installation and maintenance of a variety of manufacturing equipment ranging from hydraulic and pneumatic systems to robotic and automated systems. Additionally the program emphasizes quality control and blueprint reading to ensure products meet standards and specifications.

KCC offers a certificate and an associate degree in Industrial Electricity and Electronics, as well as the more generalized Trades certificate and associate degree geared towards company-sponsored students.

  <li><strong><a href="http://catalog.kellogg.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=18&amp;poid=1736" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Industrial Technology Certificate</a></strong> (30 credits)</li>

<h3 id="instrumentation">Instrumentation Modules</h3>
Students enrolled in Instrumentation modules at the RMTC will learn process control, measurement instrumentation, pressure measurement, force weight and motion in instrumentation, flow measurement, level measurement, temperature measurement, analytical measurement and final control elements.

View the Instrumentation cost sheets for <strong><a href="https://www.kellogg.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/SP22-Instrumentation-RMTC.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Spring 2022</a></strong> or <strong><a href="https://www.kellogg.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/SU22-RMTC-Instrumentation.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Summer 2022</a></strong> for a complete listing of modules and their costs, or contact <strong><a href="mailto:kruegert@kellogg.edu">program instructor Tim Krueger</a>.</strong>

<h2 id="sequences">Course Sequences</h2>
Unlike traditional academic courses, the offerings in KCC’s Industrial Technology program consist of short modules that are components of larger units. Modules are often less than one credit apiece and can be completed at a student’s own pace in a matter of hours or days. Units range from 1 to 30 modules, depending on the area of instruction.
