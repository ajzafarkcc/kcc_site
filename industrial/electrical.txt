<h2 id="careers">Industrial Electricity &amp; Electronics Careers</h2>
Electricians and technicians trained at KCC work for companies and private contractors in a wide variety of residential, commercial and industrial settings, including homes, schools, hospitals, manufacturing facilities, shopping centers, office buildings and apartment complexes. Industrial electricians work in manufacturing facilities on machines and automated equipment.

Possible job areas/titles for graduates with an education in Industrial Electricity and Electronics include:
<ul>
  <li>Controls technician</li>
  <li>Electrician</li>
  <li>Electrical and electronic equipment mechanic</li>
  <li>Electrical and electronic installation, maintenance and repair worker</li>
  <li>Electrical engineering technician</li>
  <li>Electro-mechanical technician</li>
  <li>Instrumentation and/or electrical technician</li>
  <li>Self-employed</li>
</ul>
With additional education after the program, opportunities include:
<ul>
  <li>Controls engineer</li>
  <li>Electrical engineer</li>
  <li>Maintenance supervisor</li>
  <li>Production supervisor</li>
  <li>Quality control engineer</li>
  <li>Sales manager</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="sequences">Course Sequences</h2>
Unlike traditional academic courses, the offerings in KCC's Industrial Electricity and Electronics program consist of short modules that are components of larger units. Modules are often less than one credit apiece and can be completed at a student's own pace in a matter of hours or days. Units range from one to 30 modules, depending on the area of instruction.

Industrial Electricity and Electronics modules carry the course designator INEL as detailed in the most recent <strong><a href="https://www.kellogg.edu/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/FA21-Electricity-1.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Fall 2021 Industrial Electricity/Electronics cost sheet.</a></strong> Units in this program include:
