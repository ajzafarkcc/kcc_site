Many piping systems are regulated by automatic controls which are accessed remotely. The Industrial Pipefitting program at Kellogg Community College prepares students to install, maintain and repair such systems.

KCC offers a certificate and an associate degree in Industrial Electricity and Electronics, as well as the more generalized Trades certificate and associate degree geared towards company-sponsored students.

<h2 id="careers">Industrial Pipefitting Careers</h2>
Pipefitters can find rewarding, long-term employment with companies and private contractors in a variety of settings, including schools, hospitals and manufacturing facilities. Pipefitters also work for utility companies, agricultural operations and municipalities.

Possible job titles for graduates with an education in industrial pipefitting include:
<ul>
  <li>Brazers</li>
  <li>Cutters</li>
  <li>Mechanical engineering technicians</li>
  <li>Pipefitters</li>
  <li>Plumbers</li>
  <li>Solderers</li>
  <li>Steamfitters</li>
  <li>Welders</li>
</ul>
With additional education after the program, opportunities include:
<ul>
  <li>Business/shop owner</li>
  <li>Maintenance supervisor</li>
  <li>Mechanical drafter</li>
  <li>Mechanical engineer</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="sequences">Course Sequences</h2>
Unlike traditional academic courses, the offerings in this program consist of short modules that are components of larger units. Modules are often less than 1 credit apiece and can be completed at a student’s own pace in a matter of hours or days. Units range from one to 30 modules, depending on the area of instruction.

Industrial Pipefitting modules carry the course designator INPF as detailed in the most recent <strong><a href="https://www.kellogg.edu/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/FA21-Pipefitting.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Fall 2021 Pipefitting Cost Sheet.</a></strong> Units in this program include:
